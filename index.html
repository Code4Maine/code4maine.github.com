Our Priorities. The things we care about.

  • It is important to us that San Diego lead the nation in efficient procurement of civic software solutions.
  • We believe that San Diegans should be able to work together create a more engaged and efficient democracy.

Works In Progress. Some Of Our 2013 Projects.

  • Catalog of San Diego Tech Resources and Datasets. List of resources that you can use to create useful and fun things.
  • Open San Diego Maps. Open Repository of Geospatial data from the San Diego Region.
  • San Diego Regional Data Library. A Library of San Diego Datasets
  • ecitizens. A Knight Foundation funded prototype development project to collect, store and analyze local-government documents and multimedia - then alert citizens when their keywords are matched.
  • San Diego Tree Map. An Open and editable map of Trees in San Diego County. The brigade will be working on helping to make this project sustainable going forward.

See our full list of projects and repositories on GitHub

Generally interested in Open Government? ... not a coder but want to help?

We are the software development group affiliated with Open San Diego, which is a larger group of San Diegans who meet regularly to share knowledge and collaborate on all sorts of projects to make our region a better place to live. Join us at one of the Meetups which are held about once a month and usually have a specific speaker or topic. Also Check out the general forum, the Open San Diego Blog and @opensandiego on twitter.

We Meet Often. Come Join Us.

View our Meetups site for our next location and time. We try to meet every few Weeks where we hack (develop) our projects, or brainstorm on current issues and activities around San Diego.

Want To Help Your Neighborhood, City or Region? Good, we could use your help.

We are a Code for America Brigade, which means we're volunteers that contribute our talents toward improving the way our local governments and community organizations use the web. Brigade is about action and you're here because you are someone who takes action. See what kind of things brigades do, or watch the inspirational TED talk given by Jennifer Pahlka, the founder of Code for America.

We Discuss Things Online. Join and Introduce Yourself.

Can't make it to a meetup? No problem. We collaborate on projects, ask for help, and post wins to our Code for San Diego forum where you can get new posts in email and reply. We welcome anyone to sign-up... you might just see something you're interested in helping with. We're also in IRC chat on freenode.net channel #codeforsandiego.
